ACCIDENT DESCRIPTION:
Upon arrival at the scene, this officer observed a two-vehicle collision at the intersection of Main Street and Oak Avenue. V1 (Honda Civic) was positioned approximately 22 feet west-southwest of its original lane, having been displaced by the impact. V2 (Ford F-150) was in the southeast corner of the intersection with significant front-end damage.

Traffic signal analysis from the city's traffic management system confirmed the following signal timing at the time of impact:
- Main Street (E/W direction): GREEN for 14 seconds prior to impact
- Oak Avenue (N/S direction): RED for 11 seconds prior to impact

V2 entered the intersection against a solid red signal.

WITNESS STATEMENTS:
Three independent witnesses were interviewed at the scene:

1. Robert Chen (driver, silver Toyota Highlander, stopped at red on Oak Ave):
   "The light was clearly red for the truck. He came through going pretty fast, at least 40-45 mph. The sedan had a green light and was just going through normally."

2. Diana Foster (pedestrian, northeast corner):
   "I was waiting to cross. The truck definitely ran the red. I remember thinking he was going way too fast."

3. Marcus Okafor (driver, eastbound Main Street):
   "The Honda had the green. The truck came flying through from Oak Avenue. The light had been red for the truck for a while."
